Pizza history

Pizza has a long history. Flatbreads with toppings were consumed by the ancient Egyptians, Romans and Greeks. (The latter ate a version with herbs and oil, similar to today’s focaccia.)

But the modern birthplace of pizza is southwestern Italy's Campania region, home to the city of Naples.

Sizes

Small pizzas average between 8 and 10 inches in diameter and will yield about six slices. Medium pizzas run 12 inches in diameter and will give you about eight slices.

Large pizzas are 14 inches in diameter and will offer approximately 10 slices.
